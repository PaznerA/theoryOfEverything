## Abstract

A program that probes quantum gravity by putting the Sorkin–Johnston (SJ) state and the Sorkin–Yazdi spacetime entanglement entropy (SSEE) on Poisson-sprinkled causal sets has, in a clean 2D setting, produced three positive results: a quantitative de Sitter entropy-area law with a constant coefficient ($S_{\rm cap}=A_{\rm horizon}/(c\,G)$, $c\approx7.57$, density- and patch-size-invariant; F-028/F-029), a numerically resolved type-III$_1\to$II von Neumann transition (three independent finite-$N$ proxies; F-015/F-019/F-032), and a continuous superradiant-exponent law that refutes a dimensional constant (F-030). This note reports, with equal weight, **three sharp negatives** that bound where the *same machinery* fails when pushed beyond the clean 2D case — each with a quantitative diagnosis of the mechanism, not merely a null. **(1) The 4D entropy-area law is genuinely absent.** The constant 2D coefficient does *not* survive to 4D: the discrete content entropy scales volumetrically ($S_{\rm full}\sim\rho^{1.0}$) while the corrected codimension-2 horizon-molecule count scales as the proper area ($\rho^{0.5}$), so their ratio drifts and no area-law constant exists (F-031); this is **not** an artifact of the non-conformal 4D scalar (a conformally coupled scalar gives an *identical* drift, F-037), and it is absent on the **variance** axis as well as the mean (order-by-disorder molecule fluctuations scale as $\rho^{0.66}$, excluding both area $\rho^{0.5}$ and volume $\rho^{1.0}$; F-038). The 2D constant is a *dimensional accident*: there the horizon is a point and both quantities scale as $\rho^{1}$. **(2) A surrogate modular Dirac carries boost structure but not temperature or metric.** Reading the SJ modular flow as Connes–Rovelli thermal time, the modular kernel on a Rindler slab reproduces the Bisognano–Wichmann boost geometry ($\rho$-invariant linear diagonal, $R^2=0.97$, cleanly failing on non-Rindler controls; F-036), but a surrogate Dirac $D_K=\mathrm{sgn}(K)\sqrt{|K|}$ built from the modular spectrum does **not** recover the absolute Unruh temperature $2\pi$ from a geometry-fixed normalization (off by 52%), and its Connes distance does **not** track causal distance (F-033). The bridge between causal sets and noncommutative geometry, via this surrogate, is a bridge of *structure*, not of temperature or of the spatial metric; the modular surrogate in $\varepsilon=\ln[\mu/(\mu-1)]$ units log-compresses. **(3) The discrete entropy coefficient is geometric, not an anomaly charge.** The dimensionless SSEE coefficient $c_{\rm EE}\approx7.56$ matches *no* pre-registered trace-anomaly rational built from the free-field central charges (the scalar target $|c/(-a)|=3$ misses by 152%; the index-protected $-18/11$ by 362%; F-039), so the exact NCG $a_4$ identity remains a question-independent index core that does not reach the entanglement line. We argue that each wall is informative: it identifies, quantitatively, the structural ingredient a genuine result would require (a curved-spacetime propagator that breaks the 2D coincidence; a non-surrogate Dirac from the geometric Killing boost with chiral grading; an anomaly-sensitive rather than cutoff-sensitive entropy coefficient). This is an internal exploratory note; the limits say so plainly, and the positives it bounds are reported elsewhere.

## 1. What this letter reports, and why a negative is worth reporting

A discrete-spacetime program inevitably produces more dead ends than results, and the dead ends are usually deleted. We report three here because each is *sharp* — it comes with a quantitative mechanism and an explicit pre-registered criterion that was missed by a large, robust margin — and because together they delineate a coherent boundary: the clean 2D causal-set/SSEE results do not naively extend to (a) the physical dimension, (b) the noncommutative-geometry spectral language, or (c) the exact conformal-anomaly content, and the *reasons* they do not are structural, not numerical.

We are explicit, throughout, about what is *not* claimed. We do not claim the crossed-product construction (CLPW, arXiv:2206.10780), the SSEE formula or its double truncation (Sorkin–Yazdi, arXiv:1611.10281; cf. Belenchia et al., arXiv:1712.04227), the area-law rank (arXiv:2008.07697), the thermal-time hypothesis (Connes–Rovelli, arXiv:gr-qc/9406019), the modular-Hamiltonian-from-correlators method (Casini–Huerta, arXiv:0905.2562), the horizon-molecule counting (Dou–Sorkin, arXiv:gr-qc/0302009), or the spectral-action $a_4$ identity (Chamseddine–Connes, arXiv:hep-th/9606001). Each negative is a statement about *our* discrete probe of these objects, on finite $N$, in a stated geometry.

## 2. Method, in one paragraph

All three results share the pipeline of the companion notes: a Poisson sprinkling of a bounded region; the retarded Green function and Pauli–Jordan operator $i\Delta$ on the causal order; the SJ state $W=\sum_{\lambda>0}\lambda\,vv^\dagger$; and, where entropy is computed, the SSEE generalized eigenproblem $W_{\mathcal O}v=\mu\,i\Delta_{\mathcal O}v$ with the Sorkin–Yazdi double truncation $\kappa=\sqrt N/(4\pi)$. The de Sitter results use the static-patch sprinkling with its $\mathrm{sech}^2$ proper measure; the modular-Dirac results use the one-particle modular kernel $K$ from $(W,i\Delta)$ on a Rindler slab. Every stochastic step is explicitly seeded; the de Sitter discreteness scale $\varepsilon\sim\rho^{-1/d}$ is fixed from the independent F-006 measurement *before* any ratio is formed (anti-circularity). The constructions preserve the causal order and the proper measure; they do **not** preserve the exact curved-spacetime two-point function — a limitation that becomes load-bearing for Wall 1.

## 3. The positives these negatives bound (stated before the negatives)

So that the negatives are read against the right baseline: in 2D the de Sitter entropy cap is a quantitative area law, $S_{\rm cap}=A_{\rm horizon}/(c\,G)$ with $c=7.57$ (CV $1.3\%$ on the committed F-028 run; CV $3.0\%$ when extended across a $5\times$ density and $3.6\times$ patch-size range, F-029), cross-HW reproduced (F-028/F-029); the type-III$_1\to$II transition fires on all three finite-$N$ proxies (F-019 in 4D slab; F-032 upgrading the 2D diamond from 2/3 to 3/3 at $\ge30$ seeds); the modular flow on a Rindler slab is a genuine single-temperature KMS flow whose boost geometry is $\rho$-invariant and fails cleanly on non-Rindler controls (F-036, positive part); and the spectral-action $a_4$ ratio is the exact, index-protected rational $-18/11$ (F-003/F-014). The negatives below are precisely the points at which these positives stop.

## 4. The three walls

### 4.1 Wall 1 — the 4D entropy-area law is genuinely absent (the 2D constant is a dimensional accident)

In 2D the de Sitter horizon is a point (codimension-2 = 0-dimensional), and the content entropy $S_{\rm full}$ and the Dou–Sorkin horizon-molecule count $A_{\rm mol}$ *both* scale as $\rho^{1.05}$, so their ratio $R=S_{\rm full}/A_{\rm mol}$ is density-invariant and the area law holds with $c=1/R\approx7.57$. We asked whether the same constant survives to the physical dimension and find it does not, by three independent routes.

**(i) Mean.** A naive 4D horizon-molecule count appeared to scale as $\rho^{1.77}$, which we traced (F-031) to a convention error: the count was over irreducible links piercing the *codimension-1 worldtube* $\{r^*=R_{\rm CUT}\}$ (in which $t$ and the transverse coordinates vary), not the *codimension-2 entangling surface* $\{r^*=R_{\rm CUT},\,t=0\}$. The corrected codimension-2 Dou–Sorkin molecule count (a new library primitive restricting straddling links to a proper tube around the fixed-time 2-surface) scales as $\rho^{0.49\pm0.01}$ — exactly the proper-area target $\rho^{1/2}$. **But** $S_{\rm full}$ scales as $\rho^{0.997}$ (volumetric), so the ratio $R'=S_{\rm full}/A_{\rm mol}^{\rm codim\text-2}$ drifts as $\rho^{+0.50}$ and no constant exists. The 2D constancy was a coincidence of the point-horizon, in which "area" and "content" share an exponent; in 4D the degeneracy lifts.

**(ii) Conformal coupling (the one unresolved caveat, now closed).** The 4D massless scalar is not conformally invariant, so one might attribute the absence to the controlled approximation (flat causal order + de Sitter measure, not the exact propagator). We tested a conformally coupled scalar ($\xi=1/6$, the operator $-\Box+\xi R$ with $R=12/\ell^2$ giving a constant $m_{\rm eff}^2=\xi R=2/\ell^2$) and find the drift exponent **identical** to the massless case: $d\ln R'/d\ln\rho=+0.386\pm0.053$ for both $\xi=1/6$ and $\xi=0$ ($S_{\rm full}$ changes by $\le0.05\%$; F-037). The absence is therefore robust physics of the flat-causal/de-Sitter-measure construction, not a conformal-weight artifact.

**(iii) Variance.** Because the *mean* count fails, we asked whether the area law lives in the *fluctuation* (an order-by-disorder count), in analogy with the cosmological-constant shot noise that survives on the variance axis where its mean prefactor was refuted (F-005/F-035). It does not: across five densities $\rho\in\{120,\dots,1920\}$ and 200 sprinklings, the molecule-count variance scales as $\mathrm{Var}(N_{\rm mol})\sim\rho^{0.656}$ with a bootstrap 95% interval $[0.575,0.745]$ that **excludes both** the proper area $\rho^{0.5}$ and the volume $\rho^{1.0}$, and the Fano factor rises from 3.7 to 5.3 (genuinely super-Poisson — the straddling links are correlated near-null configurations, unlike the clean Poisson point count) (F-038). The 4D area law is absent on the mean and the variance axes alike.

**What a positive would require.** An exact curved-spacetime de Sitter two-point function (not the flat-causal surrogate), which would break the $S_{\rm full}\sim\rho^{1}$ volumetric scaling that the surrogate inherits from the flat order. Wall 1 is, at root, the statement that the surrogate preserves the measure but not the propagator, and the 4D area law lives in the propagator.

*A distinction to avoid a skim-misread (same one draft-04 draws):* "the 4D entropy-area law is genuinely absent" refers specifically to the **de Sitter entropy-cap** area law (the $S_{\rm cap}=A/(cG)$ constant, F-031). It does **not** deny the separate truncated-SSEE **slab** area law, which *is* present in 4D — there the truncated entropy scales with the type-II rank $\sim N^{0.55}\approx\sqrt N$ (F-019), one of the type-transition proxies of the companion note. The two are different observables: F-019 (slab, present) is a rank/type signature; F-031 (dS cap, absent) is an area-constant signature.

### 4.2 Wall 2 — a surrogate modular Dirac carries boost structure, not temperature or metric

Reading the SJ modular flow as Connes–Rovelli thermal time invites a concrete bridge to noncommutative geometry: does a Dirac operator built from the modular data reproduce (a) the absolute Unruh temperature and (b) the spatial metric via Connes' distance formula? On a 2D Rindler slab, where the modular Hamiltonian is analytically the Bisognano–Wichmann boost:

**Positive (structure).** The modular-kernel diagonal $|K(x,x)|$ is linear in proper distance from the horizon with $R^2=0.97$, $\rho$-invariant to 1.7% CV, and collapses cleanly on an interval cut ($R^2=0.04$) and a shuffled-$K$ control ($R^2=0.07$). The flow is a genuine single-temperature KMS flow. The thermal-time reading holds *qualitatively*.

**Negative (temperature).** The KMS inverse temperature comes out $\beta_{\rm KMS}=1$ to machine precision — but this is a *tautology* of the Tomita–Takesaki construction, not a measurement. The non-tautological test is whether the absolute Unruh coefficient $2\pi$ (the Bisognano–Wichmann/Tolman relation $\beta_{\rm local}(x)=2\pi\,x_{\rm proper}$) is recovered from a normalization fixed *independently* from the sprinkling geometry. It is not: the best geometry-fixed route gives $9.6$ against the target $6.28$ (off by 52%); a de Sitter static-patch repeat gives the Gibbons–Hawking $2\pi$ off by a similar factor (F-036). The deficit is robust across windows and densities — a stable property of the surrogate, not a discretization noise.

**Negative (metric).** The Connes distance $d_D(x,y)=\sup\{|a(x)-a(y)|:\|[D_K,a]\|\le1\}$ of the surrogate Dirac $D_K=\mathrm{sgn}(K)\sqrt{|K|}$, computed over point pairs spanning the slab, does not track the causal/geodesic distance: the Pearson correlation is $0.32$ ($R^2=0.10$, deterministic 16-pair value), far below the pre-registered tracking threshold of $0.5$; $d_D$ is essentially flat ($\sim2.0$–$2.4$) across separations (F-033). The optimizer is verified independently on a canonical 1D Dirac chain (nearest-neighbour distance exactly 1, monotone) — the flatness is a property of the modular surrogate, not a solver failure.

**Mechanism and what a positive would require.** The surrogate Dirac is built in the modular-energy units $\varepsilon=\ln[\mu/(\mu-1)]$, which log-compress: the Unruh-law exponent comes out $0.72$ against the Bisognano–Wichmann $+1$. A genuine bridge would require a *non-surrogate* Dirac built from the geometric Killing boost generator $\xi=x\partial_t+t\partial_x$ with an explicit chiral grading $\gamma_5$ — i.e. an even spectral triple — rather than the square-root-of-modulus of the modular kernel. The causal-set $\leftrightarrow$ NCG edge, through this surrogate, is therefore a tested *informed negative* on the metric and temperature axes, with a solid qualitative positive on the boost/thermal-time axis.

> **Note (round 22, F-040 — status `confirmed-mixed-sharper-negative`).** The missing ingredient named in the previous paragraph — a geometric, $\gamma_5$-graded boost Dirac ($\xi=x\partial_t+t\partial_x$, even spectral triple) — has since been *built* on the 2D Rindler slab and is well-posed at finite $N$ ($\{D,\gamma_5\}=0$ machine-zero, $D$ Hermitian, $\pm$-paired chiral spectrum to $2.9\times10^{-15}$, unlike the chirality-free surrogate). It **resolves** the log-compression obstruction of F-036: the lawful exponent is $p_E=+1$ ($0.989$, $R^2=1.000$) rather than the surrogate $0.72$, and the absolute coefficient is $O(2\pi)$ rather than off by $4.8\times$. **But the absolute Unruh $2\pi$ still does not return $\rho$-invariantly**: the non-tautological operator route *drifts* with $N$ ($4.747\to5.647\to7.707$, $\mathrm{CV}=0.205$, not $\rho$-invariant), so the obstruction has **moved** from $\varepsilon$-log-compression onto the finite-$N$ discretization of the first-order boost generator. The verdict is therefore *sharper*, not overturned: the NCG$\leftrightarrow$semiclassical bridge remains a bridge of *structure* plus now a well-posed even triple, not a bridge of stable absolute temperature. (Parallel finite-$N$ negative, F-041: the same $\mathrm{Tr}\,e^{-\sigma D^2}$ heat trace gives $d_s=2$, but the $a_4$ rational $-18/11$ does **not** reproduce on the flat causet — the Wall-3 separation seen from the NCG side.)

### 4.3 Wall 3 — the discrete entropy coefficient is geometric, not an anomaly charge

The trace-anomaly central charges $(a,c)$ govern the universal terms of entanglement entropy (the sphere coefficient $a$ of Casini–Huerta–Myers; the 4D logarithmic coefficient $\sim c$ of Solodukhin), and the spectral-action $a_4$ ratio is the exact $-18/11=c/(-a)$ for one Weyl fermion (F-003/F-014). It is therefore natural to ask whether our discrete SSEE universal coefficient $c_{\rm EE}\approx7.56$ (F-029) is itself an anomaly-built rational — which would, for the first time, connect the index-protected NCG core to the entanglement line.

It is not. Pre-registering the candidate rationals before reading the data, we find $c_{\rm EE}=7.562\pm1.3\%$ matches none: the *direct* scalar target $|c/(-a)|=3$ misses by 152%, the secondary index-protected $|-18/11|=1.636$ by 362%, and the nearest candidates across all six reporting channels are the round-number geometric controls $8$ (off by 5.5%, still above the 5% threshold) and the Bekenstein quarter $4$ (off by 10%). A fresh 2D-diamond SSEE confirms the area law is the logarithmic CFT form $S=(c_{\rm log}/3)\ln L$ with $c_{\rm log}=0.227$, which is likewise no anomaly rational (F-039). The discrete SSEE coefficient is a **geometric**, $\kappa$-cutoff-driven quantity, not a conformal-anomaly charge. The $-18/11$ identity is connected, in the concept graph, to the trace-anomaly cluster it literally computes — but it does *not* reach the entanglement line through the discrete coefficient. It remains a question-independent index core.

**What a positive would require.** An entropy observable whose universal coefficient is anomaly-sensitive rather than cutoff-sensitive — e.g. a continuum-limit, regulator-independent universal term extracted at fixed proper geometry, rather than the discreteness-rank-set $\kappa$-truncation coefficient.

## 5. The common thread

The three walls share a structure: in each, the discrete probe reproduces the *qualitative* or *structural* content (an area-law-like cap in 2D; the boost geometry; the entropy coefficient) but fails the *quantitative*, *absolute*, or *anomaly-sensitive* test, and in each the failure is traced to a specific surrogate. Wall 1: the construction keeps the measure but not the propagator, so the 4D area law (which lives in the propagator) is absent. Wall 2: the Dirac is the square root of the modular kernel in log-compressing units, so absolute temperature and metric are lost. Wall 3: the entropy coefficient is set by the discreteness cutoff, not the conformal anomaly. The unifying lesson is that the clean 2D/structural successes of the SJ–SSEE program are necessary but not sufficient for the 4D/quantitative/algebraic claims they invite — and the gap is, in every case, a named missing ingredient rather than a vague "more compute".

## 6. Limits (read this before believing the abstract)

These are negatives of a *specific finite-$N$ discrete construction*, not no-go theorems. (a) All entropy results are on finite causal sets, where every algebra is trivially type I$_n$; "type" and "area law" are $N\to\infty$ trends measured here, not invariants. (b) Walls 1 and 2 rest on the flat-causal-order + proper-measure surrogate and the modular-square-root surrogate respectively; an exact curved propagator (Wall 1) or a geometric-boost Dirac (Wall 2) is untested and could change the verdict — that is precisely the point of naming them. (c) Wall 3's pre-registration controls anti-circularity but the candidate-rational list is finite; a different anomaly-built quantity could in principle match. (d) The Connes-distance correlation (Wall 2) is a small (16-pair) sample, deterministic but noisy; the verdict rests on its weakness ($R^2\approx0.1$), not its precise value. (e) No analytic derivation underlies any of the three. We report them because a sharp, mechanism-bearing negative bounds the program more usefully than an un-reported one.

## 7. Summary

Three sharp negatives bound the SJ–SSEE–NCG program beyond its clean 2D base: the 4D entropy-area law is genuinely absent (robust to conformal coupling and to the variance axis; the 2D constant is a point-horizon dimensional accident); a surrogate modular Dirac carries boost structure but not absolute temperature or spatial metric (the bridge to NCG is structural, not thermal/metric); and the discrete SSEE coefficient is a geometric cutoff quantity, not the trace-anomaly rational that would connect $-18/11$ to entanglement. Each names the structural ingredient — curved propagator, geometric-boost chiral Dirac, anomaly-sensitive coefficient — a positive result would require. *Forward note (round 22):* the Wall-2 ingredient (geometric $\gamma_5$-graded boost Dirac) was subsequently built (F-040) and *resolved* the log-compression obstruction (exponent $+1$, coefficient $O(2\pi)$) — yet the absolute $2\pi$ still fails to return, the obstruction having moved to finite-$N$ discretization (operator route drifts, $\mathrm{CV}=0.21$); the verdict is sharper, not overturned. The Wall-3 separation is independently confirmed from the NCG side (F-041: $d_s=2$ but $a_4\ne-18/11$ on the flat causet).
